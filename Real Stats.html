Real Stats

"Real Stats" was a project created as part of the Coughacks Hack-a-Thon, where I, a junior at the time, teamed up with four other student programmers. We had just 24 hours to come up with a prototype, design the project, document our work, and present a functional software to a panel of judges. The catch was that we had to follow a theme that was revealed to us at the opening ceremony.

Despite the time constraints and some missing features, we managed to secure second place in the teams category. Since the Hack-a-Thon, we haven't made any further progress on the project. However, this experience exposed me to every aspect of software engineering, including development, teamwork, testing, and problem-solving. It was like going through an entire software development cycle in just 24 hours.
